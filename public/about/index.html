About 3D Print Skincare

Where Beauty Meets Advanced Manufacturing

3D Print Skincare covers the intersection of additive manufacturing and personal care — the niche where a beauty brand’s CAD file becomes a production-ready facial device without six months of mold tooling, and where a formulator’s lab needs a custom patch-testing fixture that no catalog supplier stocks.

The site is published by Ben Stagl and Max Davis (ChiLab Studio, Chicago), with manufacturing expertise spanning 20 years of fabrication, SAIC professorship in designed objects, and CNC/digital fabrication at MAXCNC. Our collaborators include formulation chemists, product design studios, and contract manufacturers across the beauty supply chain.

Why 3D Printing is Transforming Skincare

The traditional beauty product development cycle — concept, CAD, tooling, sample, revision, production — takes 18–24 months and costs $50K–$500K before a single unit ships. 3D printing compresses this radically:

  • Tooling-free prototypes in 48 hours at production surface quality (Ra < 0.8 µm SLA)
  • Skin-safe materials certified to ISO 10993 for direct skin-contact applications
  • Functional validation of pump mechanisms, dispensing geometry, and grip ergonomics before committing to tooling
  • Short-run production (10–5,000 units) for indie brands, limited editions, and clinical trial packaging
  • Regulatory support — material certificates, biocompatibility data, and manufacturing documentation for FDA cosmetic device submissions

The Landscape We Cover

From gua sha tool manufacturing to microfluidic lab fixtures for permeation studies — 3D Print Skincare tracks the full spectrum: machine technologies (SLA, multi-jet fusion, binder jetting), materials (medical-grade resins, skin-safe TPU, ceramic composite), regulatory frameworks (FDA 21 CFR Part 820 for devices, EU MDR), and the brands and service bureaus driving adoption.
